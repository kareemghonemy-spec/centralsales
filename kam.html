Key Insight: Ads revenue (NCR) is outperforming GMV growth at the quarterly level (+25.4% vs +21.2%), meaning the ads monetization rate is improving. The NCR/GMV ratio expanded from 2.03% → 2.10% QoQ and from just 1.75% a year ago in Q2'25 — a significant +35bps YoY improvement. However, within Q2, May saw GMV grow faster (+7.9%) than NCR (+3.4%), while June showed NCR declined less than GMV (−1.1% vs −3.4%), suggesting ads revenue is becoming more sticky and less volatile than GMV.

Upsell breakthrough in Q2: Upsell (above-contract incremental) surged from just 2.4% of KAM NCR in Q1 to 16.2% in Q2 — a 7× increase. May was the strongest month at 20.5%. This is driven primarily by McDonald's activation and increased discretionary spend from Americana and Kitopi. June softened (+734K) suggesting upsell is still largely campaign-dependent rather than always-on.
